My Location Independence Journey

  • 2013 - Graduated UC Davis with a degree in International Relations and a minor in Psychology.
  • 2013 - Started real first job as a business analyst. Wrote out business logic in XML. Had a lot of downtime so started learning HTML/CSS at work out of boredom
  • 2014 - Built first website following online tutorials. Got a new job at a startup building white label mobile apps using a combination of scripts and tools. Learned git, JIRA, mobile app production process and submission, Apple Store rules, Google Play store rules etc etc
  • 2015 - Read Tim Ferris 4 hour work week, really started thinking about how to be location indepenedent.
  • 2016 - Got promoted to Production Support Engineer and got to work closely with product and every engineering team. Went to Amsterdam to learn more about microservices. Completed a Programming Nanodegree on Udacity.
  • 2017 - Got laid off. Attended a coding bootcamp in Medellin, Colombia. Started traveling South America and working on coding projects on the road.
